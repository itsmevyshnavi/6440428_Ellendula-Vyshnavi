Community Bulletin

The local community has come together to organize a variety of events this summer. From outdoor concerts and farmers markets to charity runs and art exhibitions, there is something for everyone. The city council encourages residents to participate and support these activities that bring us closer as a community.

In addition to scheduled events, new public art installations are being unveiled across the city parks, reflecting the vibrant culture and history of our town. These projects were funded through community grants and feature work by local artists.

Volunteers are always welcome to join neighborhood clean-ups, helping keep our streets and parks beautiful and safe. For more information and to sign up, please visit the community website or contact the local events office.
